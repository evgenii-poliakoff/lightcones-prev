Tracing out the backward light cone#

Invariance of entanglement across the backward light cone boundary#

Suppose we are interested in the dynamics only on the time interval $\left[0,T\right]$.

As discussed in the previous section The backward Lieb-Robinson lightcone, we have the backward Lieb-Robinson light cone:

The region colored with red consists of modes with are irreversibly decoupled from the local interacting site.

The total many-body state at time $t$ is $\left| \Psi \left(t\right) \right\rangle$.

We make the following bipartition:

$$ \left| \Psi \left(t\right) \right\rangle = \sum_p c_p \left| \psi_p \left(t\right) \right\rangle_{q+inside} \otimes \left| \phi_p \left(t\right) \right\rangle_{outside}, $$ (in_out_bipart)

where “q+inside” refers to the modes of the open system (atom) and the bath modes inside the backward lightcone; “outside” refers to the bath modes outside the backward lightcone (red colored area).

We can select $\left| \phi_p \left(t\right) \right\rangle_{outside}$ as orthogonal basis elements,

$$ \left\langle \phi_q \right| \left. \phi_p \right \rangle_{outside} = \delta_{qp}. $$

The corresponding wavefunctions for the inside part $\left| \psi_p \left(t\right) \right\rangle_{q+inside}$ need not orthogonal, but can be chosen to be normalized,

$$ \left\langle \psi_p \right| \left. \psi_p \right \rangle_{q+inside} = 1. $$

The many-body state state at a later time $\left| \Psi \left(t^{\prime}\right) \right\rangle$ is related by evolution operator,

$$ \left| \Psi \left(t^{\prime}\right) \right\rangle = \widehat{U}\left(t^{\prime}, t \right) \left| \Psi \left(t\right) \right\rangle. $$

Since the outgoing (outside the backward lightcone) modes have negligible average intensity of coupling, the evolution operator has the effective bipartitioning

$$ \widehat{U}\left(t^{\prime} -t \right) = \widehat{U}{q+inside}\left(t^{\prime}, t \right) \otimes \widehat{1}{outside} + O\left(a_{cut}\right), $$

that is it does not couple the outgoing mode.

Therefore, we have at a later time:

$$ \left| \Psi \left(t^\prime\right) \right\rangle = \sum_p c_p \left| \psi_p \left(t^\prime\right) \right\rangle_{q+inside} \otimes \left| \phi_p \left(t\right) \right\rangle_{outside} + O\left(a_{cut}\right),. $$ (in_out_bipart_prime)

with

$$ \left|\psi_p \left(t^\prime\right) \right\rangle_{q+inside} = \widehat{U}{q+inside}\left(t^{\prime}, t \right) \left| \psi_p \left(t\right) \right\rangle{q+inside}. $$

Comparing the equations in_out_bipart and in_out_bipart_prime, we see that this bipartition is invariant under the future quantum motion.

The coefficients $c_p$ are time independent.

Schmidt decomposition#

We compute the density matrix of the quantum field outside the lightcone:

$$ \widehat{\rho}{outside} = \textrm{Tr}{q+inside}\left[\left|\Psi\left(t\right)\right\rangle\left\langle\Psi\left(t\right)\right|\right] $$

and find its eigenvalues:

$$ \widehat{\rho}{outside} \left| \phi_p \left(t\right) \right\rangle{outside} = \left|c_p\right|^2 \left| \phi_p \left(t\right) \right\rangle_{outside}. $$

The resulting $\left| \phi_p \left(t\right) \right\rangle_{outside}$ yield the bipartition of the form in_out_bipart, with the additional property that $\left| \psi_p \left(t\right) \right\rangle_{q+inside}$ are also orthonormal. This is the Schmidt decomposition.

One can introduce a statistical cut off $\pi_{cut}$ so that we keep only a finite number of terms in in_out_bipart such that

$$ \left|c_p\right|^2 > \pi_{cut}. $$

Suppose there are $m$ such terms.

Computing the observables#

Suppose we are interested in some observable $\widehat{O}{q+inside} \otimes \widehat{O}{outside}$, and want to compute its average $\overline{\widehat{O}{q+inside} \otimes \widehat{O}{outside}\left(t\right)}$ at later time moments $t^\prime\geq t$.

Then we form the $m\times m$ matrix

$$ O\left(p,q\right){outside} = \left\langle \psi_p \right| \widehat{O}{outside} \left| \psi_q \right \rangle_{outside}. $$

We find its eigenvalues

$$ \sum_{q=1}^m O\left(p,q\right)_{outside} f^{(l)}_q = O_l f^{(l)}_p. $$

We choose a novel basis for bipartition:

$$ \left| \psi^{(O)}l \right \rangle{outside} = \sum_{p=1}^m f^{(l)}p \left| \psi_p \right \rangle{outside}. $$ (diagonalizing_basis)

Then the average becomes

$$ \overline{\widehat{O}{q+inside} \otimes \widehat{O}{outside}\left(t\right)} = \sum_p \left|c_p\right|^2 \left\langle \psi_p\left(t^\prime\right) \right| \widehat{O}{q+inside}\left| \psi_p \left(t^\prime\right) \right \rangle{q+inside} O_p $$

for all later time moments $t^\prime\geq t$.

Stochastic interpretation#

We can compute this average at all times $t^\prime \geq t$ via the following Monte Carlo procedure:

  • Given the state $\left| \Psi \left(t\right) \right\rangle$ at time $t$, we select the basis diagonalizing_basis

  • We consider $\left|c_p\right|^2$ as probabilities $P\left(p\right)$ of events $p=1\ldots m$. Each event corresponds to a collapse

$$ \left| \Psi \left(t\right) \right\rangle \to \left|\psi_p \left(t\right) \right\rangle_{q+inside}. $$

Observe that after the collapse there is no modes outside the backward light cone. So the collapse is accompanied with reduction of the Hilbert space.

  • We sample events $p$ according to their probability $P\left(p\right)$ via Monte Carlo algorithm.

  • As a result, we get samples of $\left|\psi_{p_s} \left(t\right) \right\rangle_{q+inside}$, $s=1\ldots M$, where $M$ is the number of samples.

  • Each sample is propagated to later times via

$$ \left|\psi_{p_s} \left(t^\prime\right) \right\rangle_{q+inside} = \widehat{U}{q+inside}\left(t^{\prime}, t \right) \left|\psi{p_s} \left(t\right) \right\rangle_{q+inside} $$ (between_jumps)

  • The sample average is computed as

$$ A = \frac{1}{M} \sum_{s=1}^{M} \left\langle \psi_{p_s} \right| \widehat{O}{q+inside} \left| \psi{p_s} \right \rangle_{q+inside} O_{p_s} $$

Benefits of stochastic interpretation#

  • We avoid density matrices. The latter would be obtained if we were to trace out the modes outside the backward light cone. Instead, we stay on the level of wavefunctions.

  • The proposed algorithm is massively parallel: each sample $\left|\psi_{p_s} \left(t^\prime\right) \right\rangle_{q+inside}$ is propagated independently. Different samples can be computed in separate threads, processes, or even on separate servers.

Recurrent application#

As we see from the figure bck_lc, the backward light cone shrinks as we move forward in time: new modes fall out of the light cone interior.

Then the above procedure can be applied recurrently: we get a sequence of quantum jumps:

$$ \ldots \to \left|\psi_{p_1} \left(t_1\right) \right\rangle_{q+inside1} \to \left|\psi_{p_2} \left(t_2\right) \right\rangle_{q+inside2} \to \left|\psi_{p_3} \left(t_3\right) \right\rangle_{q+inside3} \to \ldots
$$

at a sequence of times $\ldots, t_1, t_2, t_3 \ldots$. Between the quantum jumps the samples are propagated unitarily according to between_jumps.
